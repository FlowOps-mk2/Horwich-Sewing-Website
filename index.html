FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What does Horwich Sewing SK make?

We are a contract manufacturer of workwear, protective and technical garments, including PPE, fire-protective and arc-flash clothing, chemical-protection suits, military and defence garments, disposable and non-woven protective clothing.

Where are your garments manufactured?

At our factory in Holíč, Slovakia, inside the EU and NATO, with satellite capacity in Macedonia and a network of trusted outsourcers. We are UK-headquartered in Congleton, Cheshire, and deliver worldwide with no EU import duties.

Do you offer fully factored or CMT production?

Both. With fully factored production we source the materials and make the complete garment; with CMT (cut, make and trim) you supply the materials and we cut, make and trim to your specification.

What are your lead times and capacity?

Typical lead times are around six weeks. We run anything from one-off prototypes and samples to large production runs, with modular manufacturing and a trusted partner network giving us surge capacity for framework and call-off contracts.

What certifications and standards do you work to?

We are ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 14001:2015 certified by SGS, manufacture to EN ISO and customer or Def Stan specifications, and provide full SAP batch traceability. For defence supply we are progressing Cyber Essentials, JOSCAR, AQAP 2110 and an NCAGE code.

What is your minimum order quantity?

We're flexible and scale to your programme, tell us your product, volumes and timelines and we'll come back with a quote. Our modular lines and trusted partner network run development samples through to high-volume production.
